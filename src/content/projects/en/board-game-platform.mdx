A weekend-class project that grew a real engine: a LAN multiplayer platform for
three classic board games, where any seat can be taken by a human or by the AI.

The interesting part is the **Rust engine** — a local search engine reaching
depth 7 in chess and depth 4 in xiangqi within a 3-second budget, invoked by the
platform as a subprocess. Writing move generation, evaluation, and iterative
deepening by hand is the kind of low-level work that LLM-era engineering makes
easy to skip — which is exactly why I didn't skip it.

Playable over any local network; games run in the browser.
