A Monopoly-style engine built on boardgame.io where **the rules are data, not
code**: prices, rent curves, victory conditions, even the board geometry (square,
circle, hexagon, custom) live in a mod's config files. The shipped mod —
*Dominion: Multi-dimensional World Property Council* — is a sci-fi table of ten
characters, each with pixel-art portraits, six stats, passive abilities, and
full backstories.

Mechanics beyond the classic: a four-tier building system, a season cycle that
moves prices and taxes every ten turns, property trading and round-robin
auctions, mortgages with season-adjusted values, and three selectable victory
conditions. Optional **LLM-powered characters** react to game events in
persona.

The engineering spine: **278 Jest unit tests + 18 Playwright E2E tests**
covering the game mechanics — because a rules engine you can't trust is just a
random-number generator with extra steps. 2–10 players, hot-seat or online.
The whole thing wears a Game Boy Color aesthetic with three palettes and a CRT
toggle, because engines are allowed to be fun.
